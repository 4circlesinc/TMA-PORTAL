1. Scope

This policy applies to information we process through the Portal, a private, invitation- and approval-based application used by TM ANTOINE Advisory and its clients to manage matters, documents, e-signatures, scheduling, messaging, and related communications. It does not cover any third-party website or service that we do not operate, even where the Portal links to it.

2. Information we collect

Information you provide

  • Account & profile details — your name, email address, phone number, job title or organization, profile photo, and the password or sign-in method you use.
  • Content you submit — documents and files you upload, e-signatures and the information within signed records, calendar events, and messages you send through the Portal.
  • Communications — information you provide when you contact us for support or otherwise correspond with us.

Information we collect automatically

  • Usage & device data — log records such as IP address, browser and device type, pages viewed, and actions taken, which we use to operate and secure the Portal.
  • Security data — sign-in events, two-factor authentication status, and trusted-device identifiers used to protect your account.

3. Connected accounts & third-party data

You may sign in to the Portal with your Google or Microsoft account, and you may optionally connect that account to sync email, calendar, and cloud files (Google Drive, OneDrive, or SharePoint). When you do, we receive only the data covered by the permissions you grant, and only for the features you enable.

Google user data

The Portal’s use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Specifically:

We access your Google data (such as your profile, and — only if you enable sync — your Gmail messages and Google Calendar events) solely to provide and improve the user-facing features you request within the Portal. We do not transfer this data to others except as needed to provide those features, for security purposes, or to comply with applicable law; we do not use it for advertising; and we do not allow humans to read it unless you give explicit consent, it is necessary for security or to comply with law, or the data is aggregated and de-identified.

Microsoft user data

When you connect a Microsoft account, we access your profile and — only if you enable sync — your Outlook mail, calendar, and OneDrive/SharePoint files via Microsoft Graph, solely to provide the features you request. We handle this data on the same limited-use basis described above.

You can review or revoke these connections at any time from your Security settings in the Portal, or directly with the provider at myaccount.google.com/permissions (Google) or account.microsoft.com/privacy (Microsoft).

4. How we use information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, maintain, and operate the Portal and its features;
  • Authenticate you, secure your account, and prevent fraud or abuse;
  • Deliver documents, e-signature requests, notifications, and messages;
  • Sync email, calendar, and files when you enable those connections;
  • Provide support and respond to your requests;
  • Maintain audit trails and comply with our legal and professional obligations; and
  • Improve the reliability, performance, and security of the Portal.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use it for advertising.

5. How we share information

We share information only in these limited circumstances:

  • Service providers — vendors who host and support the Portal under contract, including our cloud hosting, database, file storage, and email-delivery providers. They may process data only on our instructions.
  • Within your matter — with the TM ANTOINE Advisory personnel and authorized participants involved in your matter, as needed to provide our services.
  • Legal & safety — when required by law, legal process, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of our clients, our firm, or others.
  • Business transfers — in connection with a merger, acquisition, or similar transaction, subject to this policy.

6. Storage, security & retention

Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS) and stored using reputable cloud infrastructure with access controls, encrypted object storage, and audit logging. We support two-factor authentication and trusted-device management to help protect accounts. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we work to protect your information using appropriate safeguards.

We retain information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide our services, and afterward as required to meet legal, regulatory, professional, and record-keeping obligations. When information is no longer needed, we delete or de-identify it.

7. Your rights & choices

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Access, correct, or update your personal information;
  • Request deletion of certain information;
  • Disconnect connected Google or Microsoft accounts and revoke their permissions;
  • Object to or restrict certain processing; and
  • Request a copy of your information in a portable format.

You can manage much of this from your account and Security settings in the Portal. For any other request, contact us using the details below. We will respond consistent with applicable law and our professional obligations.

8. Cookies & sessions

The Portal uses strictly necessary cookies to keep you signed in, remember trusted devices, and protect against cross-site request forgery. These are required for the Portal to function and are not used for advertising or cross-site tracking.

9. Children’s privacy

The Portal is intended for business and professional use and is not directed to children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us information, please contact us so we can remove it.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date above and, where appropriate, notify you through the Portal. Your continued use of the Portal after an update means you accept the revised policy.
